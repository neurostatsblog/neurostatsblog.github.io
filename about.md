I'm a faculty member at NYU working at the intersection of computational neuroscience, machine learning, and statistical inference. My research focuses on developing principled mathematical tools to understand how populations of neurons represent and process information.

This blog is a place to write up ideas, derivations, and informal notes that don't quite fit in a paper — technical things I want to think through carefully, connections I find interesting, and the occasional methodological commentary.

## Topics

Posts tend to cluster around a few recurring themes:

- **Gaussian processes and kernel methods** — inference, approximations, deep kernel learning
- **Bayesian hierarchical models** — structure, identifiability, computation
- **Neural population coding** — decoding, representational geometry, dimensionality
- **Dynamical systems** — Koopman operators, HMMs, latent variable models
- **Statistical learning theory** — U-statistics, bootstrap, concentration inequalities
- **Optimal transport** — geometry, generative models, applications in neuroscience
